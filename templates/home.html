Explore Chopin through works, recordings, places, and people

This website introduces the life and music of Frédéric Chopin through a digital database and a public-facing interface. It brings together selected works, recordings, scores, places, and people connected with Chopin, so that visitors can explore his world in a simple and direct way.

What you can do here

You can browse recordings, open score files, read short introductions, learn about Chopin’s friends and artistic circle, and use the search function to look for works by title, genre, key, place, year, or performer.

The site is designed both as a music resource for general visitors and as a course project built on a relational database. It connects musical information with a clean web interface, making it easier to move between biography, listening, and structured data.

Start exploring

If you are new to the site, a good place to begin is the recordings page, where you can listen to performances and open related work details. You can also read about Chopin’s life on the About page and learn more about important people around him on the Friends page.
